About Malahide

Malahide is a charming seaside village just north of Dublin, known for its picturesque marina, vibrant restaurants, and historic Malahide Castle. With its scenic coastal walks and cozy pubs, it's the perfect place to soak in Irish charm before and after the wedding. This is where Sarah's father, Paul, grew up, and where much of her Irish family still lives today. We can't wait to celebrate with you in this beautiful spot!

Things to do while in Malahide

  • Malahide Castle & Gardens – Tour the historic 12th-century castle and explore its stunning gardens.
  • Coastal Walks – Stroll along the scenic beach or take the path toward Portmarnock for gorgeous sea views.
  • Malahide Marina – Relax by the waterfront, watch the boats, or grab a coffee at one of the nearby cafés.
  • Local Pubs & Restaurants – Enjoy fresh seafood, a pint of Guinness, and live music at spots like Gibney's or Seabank Bistro.
  • Boutique Shopping – Browse unique local shops and pick up a little piece of Malahide to take home.

Things to do in Dublin

  • Grafton Street is a main thoroughfare in the heart of Dublin. This cobblestone street home to lots of shopping and pubs. There are also traditionally lots of busking musicians.
  • Trinity College Dublin is Ireland's most prestigious university, home to stunning architecture and the famous Long Room Library. The highlight is the Book of Kells, a beautifully illuminated 9th-century manuscript that showcases intricate Celtic artwork. A visit offers a glimpse into Ireland's rich literary and artistic history.
  • Temple Bar is Dublin's most famous cultural and nightlife district, known for its lively pubs, cobblestone streets, and vibrant atmosphere. It's packed with traditional Irish music, bustling bars, and quirky shops. While touristy, it's a must-visit for a taste of Dublin's energy and charm.
  • Guinness Storehouse is a must-visit for beer lovers, offering a deep dive into the history and brewing process of Ireland's most famous stout. The experience ends with a pint at the Gravity Bar, which boasts panoramic views of Dublin.
  • Kilmainham Gaol is a former prison turned museum that tells the powerful story of Ireland's fight for independence. A guided tour takes you through its hauntingly atmospheric corridors and cells, where many revolutionaries were once held.

Restaurant & Pub List

  • Marco Pierre White - A stylish steakhouse serving top-quality Irish beef and seafood with a French-inspired touch. Perfect for a special night out with great service and an excellent wine list.
  • Sole - Dublin's top seafood spot, known for its fresh oysters, lobster, and expertly prepared fish. A refined yet welcoming place for seafood lovers.
  • Elephant and Castle - A lively Temple Bar favorite, famous for its chicken wings and hearty comfort food. Expect a casual vibe, big portions, and great cocktails.
  • Cassidy's – A quirky, laid-back pub with graffiti-covered walls, board games, and a great craft beer selection. Perfect for those looking for a fun, alternative vibe away from the typical tourist spots.
  • The Brazen Head – Dublin's oldest pub, dating back to 1198, offering a historic atmosphere with live music and hearty Irish food. A must-visit for those wanting a pint with a side of history.
  • Toner's – A classic Victorian-era pub known for its cozy snug and perfectly poured pints of Guinness. A favorite among locals and even once visited by W.B. Yeats. It's very bustling, and a club-like atmostphere at night.

Other Ireland recommendations

  • The Ring of Kerry – A stunning scenic drive through Ireland's southwest, featuring rugged coastline, charming villages, and breathtaking mountain views. A must-do for anyone looking to experience Ireland's natural beauty.
  • Westport / The Wild Atlantic Way – Westport is a charming town along the Wild Atlantic Way, known for its lively pubs, scenic views, and access to Croagh Patrick. The Wild Atlantic Way itself is a dramatic coastal route stretching 2,500 km, showcasing Ireland's most stunning seascapes.
  • Adare – A picturesque village in County Limerick, famous for its thatched cottages, historic abbeys, and charming streets. Often called Ireland's prettiest village, it's a perfect stop for a relaxed stroll and a cozy meal. Home to two of Ireland's most famous hotels, Adare Manor and Dunraven Arms.
